Project Overview

A placeholder case study based on my current work shaping AI-enabled content systems across Microsoft experiences. The focus is on designing prompt structures, response standards, and evaluation methods that improve model output quality while protecting user trust.

The Challenge

As conversational features expanded, teams needed a repeatable way to produce reliable, helpful responses across products and scenarios. The challenge was reducing quality drift and ambiguity while still supporting speed, experimentation, and cross-functional delivery across engineering, design, and product partners.

My Approach

I introduced a content engineering model that treated prompts and responses as product components with standards, testing criteria, and governance checkpoints. This helped teams make better decisions earlier, identify failure modes faster, and align on what "good" looks like for AI-generated content.

  • Designed reusable prompt and response patterns
  • Defined LLM quality evaluation workflows
  • Embedded trust and ethics review guardrails

Outcomes & Metrics

The approach improved consistency and confidence in AI-assisted user experiences across participating teams. It also created a stronger foundation for model operations by linking content quality signals to product decisions, helping partners ship faster without compromising clarity, accessibility, or user trust.
