Project Overview

A deep dive into my work developing and teaching the content design curriculum for the University of Washington's Communication Leadership master's program. This involved creating course materials, lectures, and assignments focused on real-world UX writing and content strategy principles.

The Challenge

Students needed more than writing tips. They needed to learn how content decisions affect product systems, user trust, and business outcomes in real shipping environments. The challenge was building a graduate experience that balanced strategic thinking, practical tooling, and accessibility expectations within a single quarter timeline.

My Approach

I designed the course like a product sprint: clear learning outcomes, iterative critique, and portfolio-ready deliverables tied to real constraints. Each module connected user psychology, content architecture, and AI-informed workflow decisions so students could learn how to make tradeoffs with confidence.

  • Structured weekly design critiques
  • Systems-thinking content frameworks
  • Accessibility and trust-first writing standards

Outcomes & Metrics

The program produced stronger portfolio narratives and more strategic case studies from students entering tech-focused roles. Learners left with a repeatable method for solving complex content problems, and the curriculum established a clearer bridge between academic learning and enterprise product practice.
