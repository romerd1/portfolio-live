Project Overview

Building and nurturing a vibrant, cross-organizational content design community at Microsoft. This initiative focused on establishing shared best practices, creating mentorship opportunities, and fostering a collaborative environment to elevate the quality and consistency of content across diverse product teams.

The Challenge

Content designers across product groups were solving similar problems in isolation, creating inconsistent voice, duplicated effort, and uneven quality signals for users. The core challenge was aligning multiple disciplines around shared standards without slowing delivery across fast-moving product roadmaps.

My Approach

I built a cross-company operating model that combined community rituals, reusable guidance, and leadership alignment. The goal was to make high-quality content decisions easier to scale by turning individual expertise into shared systems that teams could adopt quickly.

  • Scaled a 300+ practitioner content design community
  • Published governance patterns and working agreements
  • Led cross-team workshops for decision alignment

Outcomes & Metrics

This work improved consistency across product experiences and accelerated adoption of shared content standards. Teams reported clearer decision-making, faster onboarding for new designers, and stronger collaboration between design, product, and engineering partners.
